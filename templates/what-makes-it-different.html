Why maps?

  • Almost all information has a geographical dimension, but it is very difficult to describe it in purely verbal terms.
    • Imagine being in a new town without a map. How long a textual guide would you need to find your way around?
  • Maptionnaire was born from the realization that questionnaire tools without a map are unusable for questions starting with “Where ...?”

What do questionnaires have to do with participatory planning?

From an urban planner’s point of view, participation is often driven by questions: you need to know what the locals know and think, and the best way to find out is to ask them.

Where are the most cherished places? Where is a connection needed? Which areas need renovation? Where do people feel unsafe?...

Why not only face to face?

  • Bringing residents together at workshops is time consuming and expensive, and only a handful of people get to have their say.
  • In contrast, online tools allow the participation of thousands of people at low cost.
  • Different, wider groups of people can participate online
  • High quality data gathered validates planning choices and helps discussions with the participants.
